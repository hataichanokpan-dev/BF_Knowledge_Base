## 🔬 Core Finding

### Dividend Yield Predicts Cross-Sectional Returns

> **Dividend yield has statistically significant predictive power for future stock returns in cross-sectional analysis**
>
> **หุ้นที่มี dividend yield สูงกว่า ณ จุดเริ่มต้นของปี มีแนวโน้มที่จะให้ผลตอบแทนสูงกว่าในปีนั้น โดยเฉลี่ย 3-4% ต่อปี**

**Key Discovery:**
- Dividend yield explains **significant variation** in cross-sectional returns
- The effect is **stronger over longer horizons** (1-year ahead better than monthly)
- **Mean reversion** is the key mechanism: high yields signal undervaluation

## 💡 Theoretical Framework

### Why Dividend Yield Predicts Returns

#### 1. Discounted Cash Flow Interpretation

```
Price = Σ (Expected Dividends) / (1 + r)^t

Therefore:
D/P = Expected Dividend / Price = r + Expected Growth

If D/P is HIGH:
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Either: (1) Expected returns (r) are HIGH                │
│ Or: (2) Expected growth is LOW                           │
│ Or: (3) Price is temporarily LOW (undervalued)          │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Fama-French Finding:** High D/P predicts high returns because prices are temporarily low (undervaluation).

---

#### 2. Mean Reversion Mechanism

```
Timeline of Mean Reversion:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────►
│                  │                    │                  │
  Price Declines    Dividend Yield     Investor         Price
  (Bad News)        Rises              Notices Value     Recovers
  ↓                 ↑                  ↓                 ↑
  D/P Increases     High D/P Signal    Buying Pressure   Returns Realized

Typical Reversion Period: 1-4 years
```

**Evidence:** Fama-French show predictability is stronger at 2-4 year horizons than monthly.

---

#### 3. Risk-Based Explanation

| Risk Factor | Low-Yield Stocks | High-Yield Stocks |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Business Risk** | High (growth dependent) | Low (mature businesses) |
| **Financial Risk** | Variable | Low (cash generative) |
| **Earnings Volatility** | High | Low |
| **Beta** | 1.15+ | 0.90-1.05 |
| **Duration Risk** | High (long-duration) | Low (shorter duration) |

**Fama-French Interpretation:** Higher expected returns compensate for risks that may not be captured by beta alone.

## 🔍 Regression Analysis

### Cross-Sectional Regression Results

```
Model: Rit = α + β(Rmt) + γ(DYi,t-1) + εit

Where:
  Rit = Return on stock i in month t
  Rmt = Market return in month t
  DYi,t-1 = Dividend yield of stock i at end of month t-1
```

#### Coefficient Estimates

| Coefficient | Estimate | t-Statistic | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------|-------------|----------------|
| **α (Alpha)** | 0.003 | 0.82 | Not significant |
| **β (Beta)** | 1.02 | 28.4 | Market exposure |
| **γ (Yield)** | **0.21** | **4.12** | **Significant at 1%** |

**Interpretation:** A 1% increase in dividend yield is associated with **0.21% higher monthly return** (approximately 2.5% annually).

---

### Sub-Period Robustness

| Sub-Period | γ Coefficient | t-Statistic | Significance |
|------------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| **1926-1946** | 0.19 | 2.84 | 5% level |
| **1947-1966** | 0.18 | 2.67 | 5% level |
| **1967-1986** | 0.25 | 3.45 | 1% level |
| **Full Period** | **0.21** | **4.12** | **1% level** |

**Conclusion:** The dividend yield effect is **robust across different market conditions and time periods**.

## 🎯 Practical Implications

### For Portfolio Construction

#### Strategy 1: Yield Tilt
```
Core Portfolio:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 70% Market Index (e.g., S&P 500)                           │
│ 30% High-Yield Tilt (Deciles 8-10)                         │
│                                                            │
│ Expected Enhancement: +1.0-1.5%/year                       │
│ Tracking Error: 3-5%                                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Strategy 2: Yield Momentum
```
Annual Rebalancing:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Step 1: Rank stocks by dividend yield (end of year)        │
│ Step 2: Select top 30% by yield                            │
│ Step 3: Apply quality filter (F-Score ≥ 6)                 │
│ Step 4: Hold for 1 year, then rebalance                    │
│                                                            │
│ Historical Performance: +3-4%/year vs. market              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Strategy 3: Barbell Approach
```
Barbell Portfolio:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 50% High-Yield Stocks (Deciles 8-10)                       │
│ 50% Growth Stocks (Deciles 1-2)                            │
│                                                            │
│ Rationale: Capture both income and growth                  │
│ Volatility: Lower than pure growth                         │
│ Returns: Competitive with tilt strategies                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 🇹🇭 Thai Market Application

### Thai Market Characteristics

| Factor | US (Study) | Thailand (SET) |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **Dividend Culture** | Moderate | High (property, banks, energy) |
| **Yield Distribution** | Many zero-yield | Fewer zero-yield stocks |
| **Tax Treatment** | Complex (varies) | Simple (10% WHT) |
| **Capital Gains** | Taxed | Tax-exempt (retail) |
| **Typical Yields** | 2-5% | 3-8% (higher average) |

### Adjustment for Thai Market

| Original Finding | Thai Market Adjustment |
|------------------|------------------------|
| **Target Deciles 8-10** | Target yield 5-8% (higher Thai yields) |
| **1-year holding** | Consider seasonal timing (April dividends) |
| **Quality filter** | Add RPT/governance checks |
| **Sector focus** | Focus on property, banks, REITs |
| **Tax consideration** | Net yield = Gross × 0.9 (10% WHT) |

### Thai Dividend Seasonality

```
Thai Dividend Calendar:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────►
│           │           │           │           │
  Q1        Q2          Q3          Q4
  (Jan-Mar) (Apr-Jun)  (Jul-Sep)   (Oct-Dec)
     │         │           │           │
  Announce   XD Dates    Payment     Year-End
  Dividends  (Peak)      Period      Results
     │         │
     └─────────┘
  Best Entry: Jan-Feb (before run-up to XD dates)
```

**Key Thai Insight:** Dividend yield predictive power may be enhanced by **seasonal timing** around April ex-dividend dates.

## ⚠️ Caveats and Limitations

### When Yield Strategy May Fail

| Condition | Warning Sign | Impact |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| **Yield Compression** | Market bids up all yield stocks | Premium disappears |
| **Rising Rates** | Interest rates rising | Dividend stocks underperform |
| **Growth Bubble** | Extreme growth stock euphoria | Value trap if rotate too early |
| **Sector Distress** | Yield sector in trouble | Yield looks artificially high |

### Quality Screening Required

| Risk | Screen | Metric |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Yield Trap** | Payout ratio | < 80% |
| **Dividend Cut Risk** | FCF cover | > 1.2x |
| **Financial Distress** | F-Score | ≥ 6 |
| **Earnings Decline** | Earnings trend | Positive 3Y |
