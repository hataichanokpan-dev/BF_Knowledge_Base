# Dividend Play - Crystallized Thesis

## 🎯 Core Idea

> **หุ้นปันผลที่ยั่งยืนให้ผลตอบแทนรวมสูงกว่าตลาด ผ่านทั้ง Income และ Capital Gain**

---

## 📐 The Formula

```
Dividend Play Success = High Yield × Sustainability × Growth × Margin of Safety

Where:
- High Yield = Dividend Yield > 5%
- Sustainability = Payout Ratio < 80%
- Growth = Dividend CAGR > 0%
- Margin of Safety = FCF Cover > 1.2x
```

---

## 🏛️ Three Pillars

### 1. Income (รายได้ปัจจุบัน)
| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **Dividend Yield** | > 5% | รายได้ทันที |
| **Payout Ratio** | < 80% | ยั่งยืน |
| **Track Record** | ≥ 3 ปี | ความมั่นคง |

### 2. Quality (คุณภาพธุรกิจ)
| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **ROE** | > 10% | ธุรกิจมีกำไร |
| **FCF Cover** | > 1.2x | เงินสดครอบคลุม |
| **D/E Ratio** | < 1.0 | หนี้สินไม่สูง |

### 3. Growth (การเติบโต)
| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **Dividend CAGR** | > 0% | ปันผลเติบโต |
| **EPS Growth** | > 0% | กำไรเติบโต |
| **DPS 5Y Change** | Positive | แนวโน้มดีขึ้น |

## 📐 Valuation Framework

### Dividend Discount Model (Simplified)

```
Intrinsic Value = D1 / (r - g)

Where:
- D1 = Next Year Dividend = D0 × (1 + g)
- r = Required Return (use 10-12% for Thai market)
- g = Dividend Growth Rate (use 5Y average)

Example:
D0 = 0.50 THB
g = 5%
r = 10%
D1 = 0.50 × 1.05 = 0.525

IV = 0.525 / (0.10 - 0.05) = 10.50 THB

Current Price = 8.00 THB → BUY (31% MOS)
Current Price = 12.00 THB → PASS (14% Premium)
```

### The April Dividend Season

> **60-70% of Thai dividend stocks go XD in March-April**
>
> **กว่า 60-70% ของหุ้นปันผลไทยตัดสินใจ XD ในช่วงมีนาคม-เมษายน**

#### Why April?

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **AGM Season** | Most companies hold AGMs in March-April |
| **Year-End Results** | December year-end → Q4 results in Feb-March |
| **Tax Planning** | Companies prefer to pay before mid-year |
| **Investor Expectations** | Thai investors expect dividends around Songkran |

#### XD Date Distribution (Estimated)

| Month | % of XD Dates | Strategy |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| **January** | 5% | Early birds, often banks |
| **February** | 10% | Pre-peak entries |
| **March** | 30% | **PEAK** - Final entries |
| **April** | 35% | **PEAK** - Most XD dates |
| **May** | 15% | Late payers |
| **June-December** | 5% | Irregular payers |

#### SETHD Index Behavior

> **SET High Dividend 30 Index (SETHD)** historically outperforms in Q1
>
> **SETHD มักจะ outperform ตลาดในช่วง Q1 เนื่องจาก dividend play activity**

| Period | SETHD vs SET | Explanation |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| **Q1 (Jan-Mar)** | **+1.5% to +3%** | Pre-dividend rally, rotation into yield |
| **Q2 (Apr-Jun)** | -0.5% to +1% | Post-XD adjustment, some rotation out |
| **Q3 (Jul-Sep)** | Neutral | Low dividend activity |
| **Q4 (Oct-Dec)** | Slight positive | Year-end positioning |

**Key Insight:** When SETHD begins consistently outperforming SET, it signals the start of dividend play season.

### Optimal Entry Timing

#### The "3-Month Rule"

```
Optimal Entry Window:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                             │
│  XD Date: April 15                                          │
│  ↓                                                          │
│  Entry Window: January 15 - February 28 (6-10 weeks before)│
│  ↓                                                          │
│  Run-up Period: March 1 - April 14                          │
│  (Stock price typically rises 3-5% during this period)      │
│  ↓                                                          │
│  XD Date: April 15 (Capture dividend)                       │
│  ↓                                                          │
│  Post-XD Adjustment: April 16-30                            │
│  (Price drops by ~dividend amount)                          │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Price Pattern Around XD

| Period | Price Behavior | Expected Change |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| **2-3 months before XD** | Baseline / Accumulation | 0% to +3% |
| **1 month before XD** | Pre-XD Run-up | +2% to +5% |
| **1 week before XD** | Final Run-up | +1% to +3% |
| **XD Date** | **Price drops** | **-Dividend amount** |
| **1-2 weeks after XD** | Post-XD Adjustment | -1% to -3% |
| **1 month after XD** | Stabilization | Normal trading |

**Strategy Insight:** Buy 2-3 months before XD to capture both the dividend AND the pre-XD run-up.

### Dividend Capture vs. Long-Term Hold

#### Two Strategies

| Strategy | Holding Period | Target | Risk |
|----------|----------------|--------|------|
| **Dividend Capture** | 2-4 months | Capture dividend + run-up | Tax, execution risk |
| **Long-Term Hold** | 1+ years | Income + Capital appreciation | Opportunity cost |

#### Dividend Capture Strategy

```
Dividend Capture Flow:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. Identify XD Date (Target: 6-10 weeks ahead)             │
│ 2. Verify dividend sustainability (FCF Cover > 1.2x)       │
│ 3. Entry: Buy 2-3 months before XD                         │
│ 4. Hold through XD Date                                    │
│ 5. Exit: 1-2 weeks after XD (or hold if undervalued)       │
│                                                             │
│ Target Return: 5-8% (dividend + run-up)                    │
│ Holding Period: 2-4 months                                  │
│ Annualized Return: 15-24% (if repeated 3x per year)        │
│                                                             │
│ ⚠️ Risk: Price doesn't recover post-XD                     │
│ ⚠️ Risk: Dividend cut before XD                            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Thai Tax Consideration

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **10% WHT** | Reduces effective dividend by 10% |
| **No Tax Credit** | Cannot claim credit against other income (retail) |
| **Capital Gains** | Tax-exempt (retail investors) |
| **Net Effect** | Prefer capital gains over dividends (tax-wise) |

**Tax Friction Example:**

```
Scenario: 5 THB Dividend
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Gross Dividend: 5.00 THB                                   │
│ 10% WHT: -0.50 THB                                          │
│ Net Received: 4.50 THB                                      │
│                                                             │
│ Stock Price Drop: ~5.00 THB (gross dividend amount)        │
│                                                             │
│ Recovery Needed: 0.50 THB just to break even!              │
│ = 10% of dividend value must be recovered via price gain   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Thai Strategy Adjustment:**
- Focus on stocks where dividend capture leads to price recovery (undervalued) rather than pure yield
- The 10% WHT makes dividend capture less attractive than in markets with tax credits
- Consider selling **before XD** if capital gain from pre-XD run-up exceeds net dividend

#### Property & REITs

| Characteristic | Timing |
|----------------|--------|
| **Typical XD** | March-May |
| **Entry Window** | December-February |
| **Special Consideration** | Rental income is recurring, less volatile |
| **Land Bank** | May have special dividends from land sales |
| **Payout Requirement** | REITs: >90% of profits (quarterly) |

**REITs vs. Common Stocks:**

| Factor | REITs/Property Funds | Common Stocks |
|--------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Payment Frequency** | Quarterly | Annual (mostly) |
| **Seasonality** | Less pronounced | Strong Q1-Q2 pattern |
| **Strategy** | Buy for income anytime | Time the seasonal rally |
| **Payout Ratio** | >90% required | 40-80% typical |

#### Banks

| Characteristic | Timing |
|----------------|--------|
| **Typical XD** | April-May (after AGM) |
| **Entry Window** | January-March |
| **Special Consideration** | Watch NPL announcements, CAR ratios |
| **Interim Dividends** | Some banks pay interim (Aug-Sep) |
| **Risk Factor** | Economic cycle sensitivity |

#### Energy & Power

| Characteristic | Timing |

#### Telecommunications

| Characteristic | Timing |
