## Core Papers (Must Read)

### 1. Original Momentum Discovery

**Jegadeesh, N., & Titman, S. (1993). Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers: Implications for Stock Market Efficiency.**
*Journal of Finance, 48*(1), 65-91.

**Key Findings:**
- 3-12 month price momentum predicts future returns
- "Winners" continue to outperform "losers"
- Momentum contradicts EMH (should not persist)

**Strategy:**
- Buy winners, sell losers
- Hold 3-12 months
- Rebalance monthly

**Relevance to VQM:**
- **Primary reference** for momentum factor
- Supports 6-month price momentum in VQM
- 20% weight to momentum justified by anomaly persistence

---

### 2. Momentum Persists

**Jegadeesh, N., & Titman, S. (2001). Profitability of Momentum Strategies: An Evaluation of Alternative Explanations.**
*Journal of Finance, 56*(2), 699-720.

**Key Findings:**
- Momentum not explained by risk factors
- Persists after transaction costs
- Not due to data snooping

**Relevance to VQM:**
- Validates momentum as robust anomaly
- Supports including momentum in VQM despite costs

---

### 3. Momentum in Emerging Markets

**Rouwenhorst, K. G. (1999). Local Return Factors and Turnover in Emerging Stock Markets.**
*Journal of Finance, 54*(4), 1439-1464.

**Key Findings:**
- Momentum works in emerging markets
- Similar magnitude to developed markets
- Not explained by global risk factors

**Relevance to VQM:**
- **Critical for Thai market** — momentum should work in SET
- Validates momentum for emerging markets thesis

---

### 4. Value & Momentum Everywhere

**Asness, C. S., Moskowitz, T. J., & Pedersen, L. H. (2013). Value and Momentum Everywhere.**
*Journal of Finance, 68*(3), 929-985.

**Key Findings:**
- Value and momentum work globally across asset classes
- **Key insight:** Value and momentum are negatively correlated (~ -0.6)
- Combining both improves Sharpe ratio significantly

**Relevance to VQM:**
- **Foundational paper** for VQM approach
- Justifies combining Value + Quality + Momentum
- Negative correlation = diversification benefit

---

### 5. Momentum Crashes

**Daniel, K., & Moskowitz, T. J. (2016). Momentum Crashes.**
*Journal of Financial Economics, 122*(2), 221-247.

**Key Findings:**
- Momentum crashes in market rebounds (after bear markets)
- Time-varying momentum premium
- Crashes related to market regime changes

**Relevance to VQM:**
- Risk management consideration
- Momentum may underperform in certain regimes
- VQM's Quality component may mitigate crash risk

## Momentum Factor Considerations

**Pros:**
- Well-documented anomaly globally
- Works in emerging markets (Rouwenhorst, 1999)
- Negative correlation with value = diversification

**Cons:**
- Higher turnover = higher transaction costs
- Momentum crashes in certain regimes
- Shorter-term signals (vs. long-term value)

**VQM Approach:**
- 20% weight to momentum (lower than V/Q)
- Focus on 6-month price momentum
- Combine with earnings revision for confirmation
