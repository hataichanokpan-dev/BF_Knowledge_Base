## Core Papers (Must Read)

### 1. Quality Minus Junk

**Asness, C. S., Frazzini, A., & Pedersen, L. H. (2019). Quality Minus Junk.**
*Review of Accounting Studies, 24*(1), 34-112.

**Key Findings:**
- Quality stocks outperform "junk" stocks significantly
- Quality defined by: profitability, earnings quality, low leverage, safety
- Quality premium persists after controlling for other factors

**Quality Definition (AQR):**
- High profitability (high gross margins, ROA, ROE)
- Low earnings variability
- Low leverage
- High payout ratio (safety)

**Relevance to VQM:**
- **Primary reference** for Quality factor definition
- Supports ROIC-WACC spread as core quality metric
- FCF conversion aligns with earnings quality

---

### 2. Profitability Premium

**Novy-Marx, R. (2013). The Other Side of Value: The Gross Profitability Premium.**
*Journal of Financial Economics, 108*(1), 1-28.

**Key Findings:**
- Gross profitability (Revenue - COGS) / Assets predicts returns
- Profitable firms outperform unprofitable firms
- Quality and Value are complementary factors

**Relevance to VQM:**
- Bridges Value and Quality
- Justifies ROIC as quality metric
- Supports FCF conversion quality measure

---

### 3. Cash Flow Quality

**Sloan, R. G. (1996). Do Stock Prices Fully Reflect Information in Accruals and Cash Flows About Future Earnings?**
*Accounting Review, 71*(3), 289-315.

**Key Findings:**
- High accruals → Low future returns
- High cash flow earnings → High future returns
- Market overestimates persistence of accrual-based earnings

**Relevance to VQM:**
- Supports FCF Conversion as quality metric
- Earnings quality matters for returns
- Cash flow > accounting earnings

## Quality Metrics for VQM Model

Based on literature review, supported quality metrics:

| **Operating Margin** | EBIT / Revenue | Profitability trend |

## Quality Factor Framework (AQR)

From Asness et al. (2019):

```
Quality Score = w1×Profitability + w2×Earnings Quality 
             + w3×Safety + w4×Low Leverage + w5×Payout
```

**VQM Adaptation:**
- Primary: ROIC - WACC spread
- Secondary: FCF Conversion, Debt/EBITDA
- Simpler but captures essence of quality
